How to Use This Plan
  • Sub-30 context: 5 miles in 29:59 = 5:59.8/mi. At VDOT 58, T-pace is 5:59/mi — meaning by race day you'll be running T-pace for the full race. The plan builds you from VDOT 50 → 58 over 15 weeks.
  • Workout sources: Most quality sessions are drawn directly from your running plan PDF. Three additional workout types have been added where they best serve the sub-30 goal — mile repeats, cruise intervals, and continuous tempo runs. These are labeled in each workout card.
  • Why those additions: Your race is a ~30-minute T-pace effort. The PDF covers speed (R-pace) and VO₂max (I-pace) well but has limited standalone threshold work. Mile repeats are the most race-specific I-pace interval for a 5-mile goal. Cruise intervals and tempo runs directly train the exact effort you'll race at.
  • Warmup/Cooldown: All quality (Q) days include 1.5 mi easy WU + 1.5 mi easy CD. Never skip the warmup — R and I paces are demanding on cold muscles.
  • VDOT Progression: Paces listed on each quality day reflect your current phase VDOT. Reassess every 2–3 weeks via Monday check-ins.
  • Easy days are non-negotiable: Every E-pace day must be truly conversational. These days enable the quality days.
  • Long runs on weekends: Saturday = long run. Sunday = short shakeout, always easy.
  • If you miss a day: Drop it. Never double up quality sessions. Protect the long run and at least one quality session per week minimum.
  • Taper (Weeks 14–15): Volume drops ~35–40%. Keep the intensity, cut the volume. Legs will feel surprisingly fresh by race week — that's normal and good.
